# Arena: Persuasive Writing Optimization

## Topic
Autoresearch + LLM Council is the simplest, cheapest way to achieve recursive self-intelligence.

## Target Audience
AI researchers, ML engineers, and tech-forward developers who are skeptical of AGI hype
but open to practical, empirical demonstrations of emergent self-improvement.
They value reproducibility, cost transparency, and working code over grand claims.

## Evaluation Criteria
1. **Rhetorical effectiveness** — ethos (credibility), pathos (resonance), logos (logic) in balance
2. **Audience-specific framing** — addresses the skeptic's real objections (hype fatigue, metric gaming, cost)
3. **Structural clarity** — clear thesis, supporting evidence, counterargument, call to action
4. **Evidence quality** — specific and accurate data points, concrete analogies, working demos
5. **Intellectual honesty** — acknowledges limitations; does not overclaim

## Constraints
- Maximum 1500 words
- No fabricated statistics or citations
- No logical fallacies
- The argument must be self-demonstrating: this system IS the proof of its own thesis

## Exploration Directions
Try different improvement strategies each iteration. Examples:
- Open with a concrete cost comparison (dollar amounts, experiment counts)
- Lead with the "recursive" angle: the system improving its own argument is proof of concept
- Use the Shopify case study as a real-world analogue
- Address the "AI judging AI is circular" objection head-on
- Reframe "recursive self-intelligence" as "autonomous empirical iteration" for skeptics
- Restructure as a numbered argument with specific evidence per claim
- Open with a provocation: "You don't need AGI. You need a loop and a metric."

## NEVER STOP
Run continuously. Each iteration should attempt a strategy not yet tried.
Never ask for permission. Never wait for human input between iterations.
